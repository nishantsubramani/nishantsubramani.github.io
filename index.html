I am a predoctoral researcher at The Allen Institute for Artificial Intelligence on the AllenNLP team, where I work with Matt Peters on controllable and efficient text generation primarily by steering language models. I also collaborate with Margaret Mitchell on personal information in large web corpora. I am also an NLP researcher affiliated with Masakhane, an open source and distributed research effort for NLP for African languages.

Previously, I spent time in industry working on controlling language models, document understanding, optical character recognition, fake speech detection, and speech syntehsis at different companies in both an applied and research context. Before this, I completed an MS in Computer Science at the Courant Institute at NYU in the CILVR group focusing on deep learning applied to natural language processing. I also hold a B.A. in Statistics and M.S. in Computer Science focusing on machine learning and natural language processing from Northwestern University.

Broadly, my research interests are:

  • Natural Language Processing
  • Controllable Text Generation
  • Efficient NLP
